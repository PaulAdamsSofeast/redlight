⚠️
700–770nm — The Dead Zone. Wavelengths in this range have limited biochemical activity and minimal absorption by cytochrome c oxidase — the key mitochondrial enzyme that drives photobiomodulation. These wavelengths produce little to no therapeutic effect. Any device heavily marketing them should be treated with scepticism.

Hair loss / regrowth 660nm Strong evidence; included in 2025 consensus review
Deep organ / vascular 940nm Medical / clinical devices only
Best all-round device choice: Dual-wavelength 660nm + 850nm — covers skin health and deep tissue in one panel, and is the most evidence-backed consumer combination available. For specialist use, add 670nm (eyes/brain) or 810nm (neurology/thyroid). The differences between 810nm, 830nm, and 850nm are subtle — any NIR wavelength in that range treats deep tissue effectively. Avoid devices marketing wavelengths in the 700–770nm dead zone.
